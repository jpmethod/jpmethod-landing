About the method

Short daily practice, small steps, answers at the back — and a level band on every workbook so you know where to start.

How the workbooks work

Every JP Method workbook has the same shape: ten sections of twenty short practice sheets, with the full answer key at the back. Each sheet takes about ten minutes. A section introduces one small idea and practises it until it sticks, and each new section builds on the last — steady, unhurried repetition rather than long worksheets.

You can also download any single section as its own small booklet from the workbook's page.

Three ways to print or read

Every workbook page offers the same book three ways, so it works however you like to read or whatever printer you have:

Print & fold into a book (A4 booklet). Print double-sided (flip on the short edge) and fold the stack down the middle — it reads like a real booklet. Needs a printer that can print on both sides.

Print at home on any printer (A4, single-sided). No double-sided printer needed. Each A4 sheet holds two worksheets side by side; print it single-sided and work straight through, or snip down the middle for loose pages.

Read on a tablet (single-page PDF). One worksheet per page, A5 — comfortable on a screen, or print it one worksheet per sheet.

Whichever you choose, the answer key is always at the back.

What the levels mean

Each workbook carries a level band — Level A, Level B, Level C… — which is its step on the ladder within its own subject. Level A is the entry point; each following level assumes the ones before it. On the homepage, every subject's shelf is ordered from Level A upwards, so reading a shelf left to right reads the ladder bottom to top.

Levels are not tied to age or school year — children start wherever the work feels comfortable. A good rule: start at Level A, and if the first sections feel easy, let them run through the pages quickly and move up a level. The early pages of every workbook are deliberately gentle; confidence first, then difficulty.

Levels also don't compare across subjects — Level B in Spanish says nothing about Level B in Mathematics. They are separate ladders.

The whole catalogue on one page: the learning map (A3 landscape PDF) shows every workbook by subject and level — print it and tick books off as you climb. Also available as a PNG image.
